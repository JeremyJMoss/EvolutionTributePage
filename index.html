A Tribute to the Mitsubishi Evolution

Click Here for more info
  • October 1992 – January 1994:

    The Mitsubishi Evolution I was the first generation lancer to be produced with the evolution naming. It was a phenomenal car sporting the famous 4g63 engine. And an outstanding AWD system.

  • January 1994 – February 1995:

    The new and improved Mitsubishi Evolution II was released it had handling improvements over its younger sibling and increased power output. It also had a larger rear spoiler for that extra downforce at higher speeds.

  • February 1995 – August 1996:

    The Mitsubishi Evolution III was released with slight tweaks to the already powerful Evo II. including better cooling for the radiator, intercooler and brakes based on new nose moulding to allow more air flow. with a new compressor housing size and compression ratio for the 4G63 which continued to improve on peak power performance.

  • August 1996 – January 1998:

    The Mitsubishi Evolution IV was born and with it a completely new platform. the engine was flipped 180 degrees to reduce torque steer and the whole shape and look of the Evo changed.The Evo was also heavier than its earlier models but this was countered by adding a twinscroll turbocharger to improve response and increase power yet again.

  • January 1998 - January 1999:

    The Mitsubishi Evolution V had not much difference to the Evolution IV. There were improvements to the Recaro seats and a slight increase in torque. the engine was also strengthened in a few places to increase reliability. The body kit was upgraded to add flared guards at the rear and some slight beautifying.

  • January 1999 – August 2001:

    The Mitsubishi Evolution VI had upgraded cooling and engine durability with a larger intercooler, larger oil cooler and new pistons. But the most exciting thing of all was in December of 1999. they released the Tommi-Makinen edition Evo. Named after the famous rally driver who had led the Evo to greatness in the rally scene.

  • August 2001 - January 2003:

    Mitsubishi's next shake up to the Evolution platform was the Mitsubishi Evolution VII. This was a much heavier version of the wonderful Evo VI but came with multiple chassis tweaks to improve grip and handling. the biggest change to this model was the adding of AYC (active centre differential) and a more effective limited slip differential. Torque and power output were also increased. this model also added the auto drivetrain model to the mix.

  • January 2003 – March 2005:

    The Mitsubishi Evolution VIII had small improvements over the evo 7 including Brembo brakes and Bilstein shocks to help with the increased strain of the rally scene. This model also came out with the MR edition which had light-weight aluminium panels to reduce weight and increased power to weight ratio. Improvements were also made to Mitsubishi's AWD system. the Mr edition was fitted with a 6 speed transmission unlike the rest of the models in this line up that sported the 5 speed.

  • March 2005 - January 2007:

    The engine in the Mitsubishi Evolution IX got a nice overhaul with MIVEC(Mitsubishi Intake Valve Electronic Control) which allowed control by the ECU for the intake camshaft allowing more duration of intake valve opening at higher RPM. This and a revised turbocharger would cause the Evolution IX to achieve the highest ;power levels seen in a production 4G63 engine. The Evo IX also sported a less "nosey" front bar. which was appreciated by alot of car enthusiasts that also liked the look and shape of the Evo VIII.

  • October 2007 - May 2016:

    The Mitsubishi Evolution X was the final edition in the Evolutions life. This vehicle was the only in the evo's histroy to not include the famous 4G63 engine. Instead this model was fitted with the 4B11 which did not have a cast-iron block like the 4G63 but instead has an alloy block. this made the engine weaker than the 4g63 and couldn't stand up to higher boost levels that the tuning scene required. The final edition Evo was also a lot heavier than the previous generations. This caused this vehicle to "not hit the mark" as alot of car enthusiast would say. Also this vehicle did not come out in manual but sported a 6 speed SST gearbox which was a great edition and allowed for faster shift times but alot of car enthusiasts prefered a manual transmission like most of the models before.

Additional Information

In the end the Mitsubishi Evolution was a legacy of the Mitsubishi rally team and thrust them forward into the performance car scene. Sadly after the Mitsubishi Evo X the demand for suv's and non performance cars won out for mitsubishi and the Evo became one for the history books. The Evolution will always remain in the hearts of the Car lover community as one of Mitsubishi's engineering marvels and ahead of it's time in regards to performance and reliability.
